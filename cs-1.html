BIA Website Redesign

Raising Publicity Through Brand Identity

↓

Requirements

  • User Research
  • Problem Statement & Insights
  • Ideation & Brainstorming
  • Design System
  • High-Fidelity Prototype

Constraints

  • 3 weeks
  • Focus on UI / Design System
  • Prioritizing IA

The Problem

    BIA is a non-profit organization in Minnesota driven to raise Brain Injury awareness throughout the state, and help affected families and individuals. We began by interviewing users and the stakeholder to identify their goals, and attempt to solve them with a website redesign.

    We hypothesized that donations were a major factor for the organization’s performance. We could streamline that user flow in our redesign, and measure success by the increase in donations.

The Stakeholder

“We could lose every donation we had, and it would be a blip.”

After interviewing the stakeholder we needed to adjust our approach. Only $70k of a $5.5million budget comes from donations...

The User

Other comments from the stakeholder:

  • The website’s main purpose is “base information” about brain injuries.
  • Volunteers and members rarely return “when they don’t need us anymore.”

We would not, however, be able to solve the issues of volunteer retention and publicity by increasing donations. What could we do, within the scope of our redesign, that would increase return volunteers and public awareness?

User Interviews

Since donating is not an impactful user flow for the organization, we prioritized qualitative research in relation to first-time volunteering and event participation.

I think continued outreach is huge. - Leah

We found that users like to see that a nonprofit is trustworthy and takes action. If they aren’t able to see that the cause is legitimate, or if their time will be wasted, they won’t volunteer or participate.

Ideation

In 5-second usability tests of the current sites, users commented that it would need to be “cleaned and polished” in order to be useful. We risk them leaving the site if they feel it’s out-of-date or disorganized.

Additionally, 5/5 interviewees said that they try to verify the legitimacy of a non-profit before spending time with them. If the users don't see that BIA is a worthy organization, they won't participate in events and give the organization the popularity it needs to gain funding.

The Solution

  1. Revised Information Architecture and user flows focused on learning more about Brain Injuries
  2. Aesthetic and minimalist design consistent with brand identity, mission, and values
  3. Meaningful media and interactions; strong UX writing to communicate the user’s impact

Revised Information Architecture

If users can easily find the information they need to understand BIA's mission, and also verify the organization’s legitimacy, they’re more likely to have confidence in them. Since they see that the cause has an impact, more users will sign up to volunteer or participate in events.

We designed a streamlined user flow to their 501(c)(3) and board of directors. We also wrote a condensed mission statment to include in the hero section of the homepage.

Aesthetic and Minimalist Design

If the page structure were too saturated with information, users may feel the site is solely information based, even though the goal is to encourage voluneering and publicity.

The design should be simple and down-to-Earth. This will communicate that the organization is trustworthy and has clear motives. The imagery and content strategy show that the organization is a community which lives outside of the website.

Meaningful Media & Interactions

If the website shows active language and media which encourages the user’s involvement, they’re more likely to have an emotional response and a personal connection with the cause.

Since interviewees usually get involved with a non-profit because of a personal connection, we suggest sending confirmation emails manually to volunteers. Their Facebook page, however, already shows how active they are. To break the ice with first-time visitors, we embedded a feed before the footer of the homepage.
