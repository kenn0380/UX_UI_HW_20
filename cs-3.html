Tribi Mobile App

Simultaneous Group Payments

↓

The Brief

Addressing the everyday need of group payments, we prototyped and tested a mobile app designed to enable users to make a payment as a group.

Essential steps included competitor analysis, to ensure we solve a need not already addressed by other apps; and user research, to affirm the scope of the need itself.

User Research

We wrote a goal-oriented user research plan for a survey and interviews before fully definining the problem of group payments. Key questions were:

  • “Tell me about the last time you contributed towards a group expense."
  • “Imagine that you pay as a group again today, [redescribing their scenario] how would you want it to go?"
  • “Tell me about the worst experience you’ve had paying in a group."

User Interviews

In user interviews, participants made the following comments:

  • “I'm 100% the person who forgets to Venmo people”
  • “It can get kind of awkward sometimes.”
  • “Afterwards I was, like, a little uncomfortable.”

71% of survey participants stated they don’t have a tool to help them split group payments

Competitor Analysis

    We noticed some competitors had distinctive features which satisfied the need to pay conveniently on a web browser without downloading the app, and returning funds if the goal was not reached. Through our feature prioritization matrix, we decided to go even further by getting all the contributions upfront before the purchase is made, since users expressed the need for knowing from the get go how much things would cost.

Wireframes & User Flows

    Our group created 4 different user flows because we wanted to base it off different scenarios. Each flow gives a different point of view as if you were the user:

  • Creating a group to make a payment
  • Sending a link to invite friends
  • Paying simultaneously for a purchase

Lo-Fi Usability Testing

  • Moved the sharing option towards the end of the payment process
  • Users were unclear with what some titles meant, adjusted section titles
  • Consistency, tool tips, notifications
