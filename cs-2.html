The Problem

  • Aspects of the IA not effectively labelled
  • User flows longer than necessary
  • Lack of consistency and flexibility in the UI
  • Content Strategy (content not accomplishing goal or inconsistent with branding)
  • Typography and color scheme did not meet AAA standards, and were not consistent through the entire site.

Attention to UI Heuristics

  • Visibility of System Status: e.g. no selected states for menu items
  • Inconsistent menu titles
  • Colors and fonts often not AAA compliant.

The User

    Since ED is primarily an information website, our goal was to understand the user’s mental mapping of the content.

    We also paid special attention to their reactions to the website’s micro-interactions and visual design.

Usability Testing

In analyzing our test results, some key items stood out:

  • Only 56.2% of tasks were fully accomplished.
  • Users were unaware that they were being taken away from the Dept of Ed website when clicking on a link
  • No users were able to use the search bar successfully.
  • No users were able to find school-specific programs / grants.

Test Results

Other qualitative findings showed that that the site’s IA did not match the user’s mental model:

  • Ambiguous titles
  • Too much / too little content on one page

The Solution

We designed a moodboard to brainstorm ideas for the visual design:

  • Clean, professional, objective
  • Consistent look and feel
  • Prioritizing accessibility

Navigation

In order to see improved success rates with navigation tasks, the IA needed to be restructured. Much of the content needed to be recategorized or renamed entirely.

Testing the New IA

In a second round of usability testing, we found that users could more easily navigate the home page and find the information they needed.

We did find that we had to make some adjustments to some small items, and in doing so, improved the user experience:

  • "Reliable, but not as much info as expected"
  • Consistent look and feel
  • Prioritizing accessibility

Iterations

Mobile Iterations:

  • Edited mobile quick links frame styles
  • Removed the photos and buttons in our original style, and added a line to differentiate the main menu item from the sub menus below.
  • Prioritizing accessibility

Style Guide Iterations:

  • Adjusted the color scheme of the different button states from the mid-fidelity prototype
  • Changed the desktop header and footer to help users distinguish header items from the utilities
